Q & A

What is this flag nonsense?

They're from the International Code of Signals and can be used for communication between boats. They represent letters of the alphabet and also sometimes have a specific meaning (like when you enter foreign waters, you fly the Quebec to indicate that you need to clear customs). Certain combinations of flags have additional meanings, many of which are rather grim. Like India Tango apparently means "I am on fire." I can just see myself sitting calmly at the helm, asking my navigatrix to grab the flags to indicate my state of conflagration. Anyway, they're rarely used by recreational sailors, except for Quebec and possibly Alpha, which means "diver down" everywhere except in America and on Van Halen albums.

Why are these flags so small?

I made them to fit inline with small text on my boat log, but most of them weren't being used. I thought it would be fun to do something with them. See their meanings here.

I'm high tech and stuff, and noticed that you are using Bootstrap. Isn't that overkill?

Yeah, probably. Matt made me do it.

Why is this called "Son of a Flag?"

My wife's grandmother used to say it when censoring herself from using the b-word. I wanted to call it "Self Flag-ellation" but that's gross and difficult to misspell properly for the best effect.
